Ed Benedict

18,000 sq ft of street skating with ledges, edges, stairs, rails, and banks. By using recycled and/or sustainable materials in its construction, and with its native landscaping and on-site stormwater treatment, this site is considered to be the first environmentally sensitive skate plaza ever constructed. Tread Lightly is a multifaceted art installation by Dan Garland, integrated into the skate plaza. It is meant to provoke thought regarding the intersection between natural and man made environments, and points towards a search for balance and sustainability. -Portland's Parks and Recreation

The "big three" is a 3 block that is the same size as ed's 5 stair and is a good way to pratice landing with some impact for intermediate skaters. For advance skaters they can challenge themselves by trying to ollie up the 3.

"Glenidict" is a skate video showing the features that Glenhaven and Ed Bennidict have to offer. The Skaters in this video are doing very advaced tricks.

This skate montage was filmed entirly in Ed. They show off a Fakie Back Side Bigger spin on the 3 block as their opening trick, an insaine trick that looked effortless.

The 5 stair at Ed has a rail in the middle and two hubbas on each sides. Intermediated skaters can practice tricks over the stair set. Advance skaters can go for grinds or slides on the features.

The 3 stair has a rail with a bank on the other side. It is perfect for learning how to do grinds on a slanted rail with a low risk factor because of how close to the ground it is. It is still a challenge to hit, but if you mess up you have hope of catching your fall.
